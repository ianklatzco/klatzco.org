Elect Craig Klatzco

 

Supporting Local Businesses
As a Lincolnwood business owner for the past 40 years, I directly understand the challenges of operating a business. Whether you agree with all our Village ordinances or not, most would agree that they should be administered fairly and without bias. The current board has approved exemptions for some businesses while forcing others to comply with special provisions above and beyond requirements. As trustee, I am committed to treating all businesses with respect and doing everything I can to encourage a stronger business community. I believe that when businesses succeed, theVillage benefits the greatest.


Fighting for Lower Taxes and Fees
While our Village has long touted in their mailings that Lincolnwood has one of the lowest tax rates in the area, what they haven't talked about is the ever increasing list of fees. Our Village builds its coffers not only from our property and sales taxes but they have long collected fees on our phone, cable, water and electric bills. In fact, they recently added a sewer fee and a red light camera to generate even more revenue. As trustee I will seek to reduce the cost of government and provide more transparency to our taxes and fees.


Observing Fiscal Responsibility
I do not believe that local government needs to have aggressive "tax-and-spend" policies to pursue the interests of the village; rather our actions should favor fiscal responsibility. Investment in our village is important but financing projects with bonds needs to be seriously reviewed, lest we leave our children with our debts. As a businessperson, I understand the value of a dollar. Under my direction, the library has enjoyed a sound fiscal policy. As your village trustee, I will ensure that the village operates with sound budgetary management and fiscal responsibility.


Bringing Economic Development with a Vision
I look up and down Lincoln and Touhy Avenues and see abandoned properties and unsustainable businesses. While other communities are courting upscale, destination businesses like Trader Joe's, we have brought in more dry cleaners and banks than any village could desire. Yet we are sending long time businesses packing. Our Village is on the cusp of a major redevelopment opportunity at the Purple Hotel property. Economic revitalization of this dilapidated property is long overdue but redevelopment must involve proper planning, design and execution. As trustee I will ensure there is a long-term sustainable vision, compatibility with the surrounding community, and a plan that builds our tax base and creates jobs.


Improving Relations with the School and Library Districts
Last year our Village made headlines in the press with troubles on our School Board. In addition, the Village is currently contemplating the passage of the largest TIF district in Village history that could seriously impact future revenue to the library and school districts. Now more than ever we need a trustee that considers and works with all of the taxing districts in the Village. As a substitute teacher at SD74 and because of my elected office at the library, I understand better than any other candidate how to fix this broken relationship.


Increasing Communications with Residents
Our current trustees are reluctant to televise all the meetings in the Village. For example, in a February Committee of the Whole meeting they discussed this topic and decided not to do anything until after the election. Why is this? It was stated that if residents are interested they can attend in person. Maybe they don't understand that meetings at 6 PM are inconvenient to attend when you are still commuting home from work, or picking up your children from after school activities, or making dinner for your family. As trustee I will fight to ensure all public meetings are available to the entire public, whether televised, streamed online, or otherwise.
